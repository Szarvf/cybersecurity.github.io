What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack that tricks individuals into revealing personal information (passwords, credit card numbers, personal data) by impersonating trusted sources (banks, colleagues, services) using emails, SMS (smishing), or phone calls (vishing). Phishing pages are often mimicing real websites and asks victims to log in and enter data. Common goals of phishers include credential theft, financial fraud, installing malware, or gaining network access. Attackers craft believable messages (urgent tone, fake invoices, password-reset prompts) to make users take action immediately. Phishing can be highly targeted (spear phishing) using personal details to increase trust of users.
