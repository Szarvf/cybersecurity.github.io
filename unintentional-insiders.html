Unintentional Insiders

An unintentional insider is an employee that accidentally exposes sensitive information or creates security risks without malicious intent.

Mistakes like sending confidential emails to the wrong person, misconfiguring systems, or losing devices with sensitive data can still lead to financial loss, data breaches, or operational disruption if handled incorrectly.

Human error and lack of awareness about security protocols is the main one however poorly designed systems, and overworked, undertrained staff can also make mistakes easy to occur.

Similarly to malicious insiders they can monitor unusual activity or access patterns, make alerts for risky actions (like uploading files to unsecured locations), and have regular audits to teach employees about sensitive data handling.

They can provide ongoing security training and awareness programs, implement automatic safeguards (encryption, acess controls), simplify process to limit human error, and encourage a positive work environment where mistakes can be reported safely.

They can be equally or even more severe than malicious insider threats because the damage can be accidental but still significant. Prevention relies heavily on training, awareness, and process design.
