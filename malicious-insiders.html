Malicious Insiders

A malicious insider is someone (employee, contractor, trusted individual) that delibirately exploits their access within an organisation and takes data for personal gain or to cause harm.

They can do numerous things including steal sensitive information, sabotage systems, delete data, or bypass security protocols. Damage can either be financial, reputational, or operational.

Things such as personal financial gain (selling data, fraud), revenge against their employer, ideological or political motives, or coercion by external attackers are a few of the main reasons however there are further possibilities.

Firstly they can monitor their employees and check for unusual access patterns or behaviour using their security analytics. They can also implement least-privelege access policies such as operational security (OPSEC) protocols. Conducting regular audits and security awareness trainings can also be done to ensure employee reliability.

They can restrict access to sensitive data based on job role, enforce strong authentication policies and monitoring, promote a positive work environment to increase employee's headspaces, and can also have clear reporting channels for suspicious activity.

Not necessarily, some insider threats can be caused on accident due to mistakes or negligence rather than intentional harm. Training and clear policies can reduce accidental incidences from occuring.
