How to Protect Yourself

Strong Passwords

Use long, unique passwords and avoid reusing similar/same ones for different things.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Add an extra step to logins where you can such as a code, fingerprint, face ID, to boost security.

Safe Browsing

Be aware, online. Avoid suspicious links, pop-ups, or downloads. Always check the actual link before relying on a website.

Backup your Data

Save data on a regular basis to either an external drive or cloud storage in case of loss or ransomware.

Software Updates

Keep applications, devices, and systems updated to the newest version to improve security weaknesses.

Device Protection

Use antivirus software, firewalls, and lock screens where possible to protect devices.

Be Aware of Phishing

If something is too good to be true, chances are, it is. Watch out for emails or messages asking for sensitive information.

Social Media Safety

Limit personal details accessible online and adjust your privacy settings.

Wi-fi Security

Use strong passwords for Wi-FI and avoid public networks without using a VPN.

Report Unusual Activity

If something looks or feels off, report it quickly. Don't be afraid even if there's nothing wrong. It's important to be safe than sorry.
